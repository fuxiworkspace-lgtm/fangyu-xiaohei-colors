## Give Every Xiaohei a Job.

Fangyu Xiaohei Colors is a character-routing Skill for Chinese content illustration and AI image generation.

It turns one fixed capsule-shaped Xiaohei into an on-demand visual team: Black carries the main story, Red raises the alarm, Blue connects the system, Green repairs and stabilizes, Yellow lights up opportunity, Gray carries fatigue and waiting, and White represents blank states.

Color is not a skin. It is a narrative function.

## The idea in one line

```text
One fixed silhouette + distinct colors + clear roles + physical collaboration
= a more expressive and human Xiaohei visual language
```

The Skill protects three things:

- Every character remains the same capsule-shaped Xiaohei, without costumes or mascot redesigns.
- Every color carries a specific narrative function instead of decorative color.
- Character count is routed from the input. Simple scenes stay simple; full-family scenes are explicit exceptions.

## Why use it

Many ideas are easy to understand in words but difficult to express in one image.

“Risk is rising,” “a system is entering the workflow,” “the project is stuck halfway,” “the team is repairing a broken connection,” or “an opportunity has just appeared” can become a small physical scene with relationships, tension, and cause-and-effect.

The Skill decides:

- how many Xiaohei should appear;
- which colors belong in the scene and which should stay out;
- what each character is responsible for;
- how they push, pull, twist, hold, repair, connect, or light up a real object;
- how to translate an abstract idea into a concrete visual metaphor;
- how to produce a reusable image-generation prompt.

## Adaptive character routing

Five characters are not the default answer. The Skill chooses the smallest sufficient set:

```text
One situation / one action       -> 1 character
One relationship / one tension   -> 2 characters
Three dependent functions        -> 3 characters
Complex conflict / collaboration -> 4 characters
Explicit family portrait / sheet -> 5 or more characters
```

The rule is simple: remove a character. If the scene still expresses the same meaning, that character should not appear.

This keeps simple images spacious and readable, while allowing complex stories to grow into a real ensemble.

Each character can also move through low, medium, high, and closing tension states. Show tension through taut strings, tilted objects, blocked doors, overloaded containers, broken bridges, or unfinished papers rather than exaggerated facial expressions.

## The Xiaohei family

| Character | Narrative function | Typical scenes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Black | Main subject, user, default operator | Work situations, ordinary frustration, story spine |
| Red | High energy, attention, pressure, heat | Alarm, conflict, competition, market heat, China-market up days, celebration |
| Green | Flow, recovery, connection, vitality | Repair, trust, growth, passing, China-market down days, relationships |
| Blue | Reason, structure, order, infrastructure | Systems, tools, code, data, rules, monitoring, automation |
| Yellow | Activation, reminder, possibility, turning point | Opportunity, ideas, launch, warm-up, invitation, breakthrough |
| Gray | Friction, low energy, transition, aftermath | Fatigue, waiting, being stuck, hesitation, review, delay |
| White | Space, unknown, pause, undefined possibility | Blank state, reset, pending input, openness, a new beginning |

Colors are stable character temperaments, not single fixed labels. The scene meaning is selected from the input. If a character has no irreplaceable job, it stays off stage.

### Expressive range

Use this three-layer mapping for every selected color:

```text
Color temperament -> scene meaning -> physical action
```

Red can represent danger, pressure, heat, competition, celebration, or an up day in the Chinese market context. Green can represent repair, trust, growth, connection, or a down day in the Chinese market context. Blue can move between systems, rules, data, monitoring, and infrastructure. Yellow can move between opportunity, reminder, launch, hope, and a turning point. Gray can express fatigue, delay, ambiguity, aftermath, or slow review. White can express blankness, reset, unknown possibility, or waiting for input.

## Capsule character lock

Every color shares the same visual identity:

- one continuous upright capsule body, with no separate head and torso;
- two small clean white oval eyes and a calm, slightly dazed expression;
- very thin hand-drawn arms and legs that physically interact with real objects;
- matte tactile texture, never glossy plastic mascot rendering;
- no complex clothes, hats, animal features, oversized heads, or exaggerated expressions.

Only the body color changes. Silhouette, proportions, eyes, limbs, expression, and physical-action logic remain fixed.

## Quick start

After installing or referencing the Skill, call it like this:

```text
Use $fangyu-xiaohei-colors to route the right Xiaohei colors for this content, decide how many characters should appear, define their roles and physical collaboration, and produce a ready-to-use image prompt.
```

For example:

```text
Use Fangyu Xiaohei Colors to turn “global stock markets are falling” into a 16:9 illustration that ordinary investors can emotionally relate to.
```

The Skill handles content extraction, character-count routing, color selection, role assignment, real-object metaphor, physical actions, and prompt design.

## Design principles

- Let color carry meaning and let action carry the story.
- Use physical objects to show tension, escalation, repair, and release.
- Dispatch the fewest characters needed, then give each one an irreplaceable job.
- Make characters work on one shared real object.
- Keep the scene readable in three seconds, with details that reward a closer look.
- Preserve whitespace, restraint, and quiet absurdity. Do not turn the image into a PPT or a mascot lineup.
